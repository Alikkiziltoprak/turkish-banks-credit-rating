## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Turkish banks demonstrated resilient financial performance in 2025, navigating a complex macroeconomic environment characterized by elevated inflation, monetary policy normalization, and persistent external imbalances. The sector's aggregate capital adequacy remains above regulatory minimums, asset quality metrics have held broadly stable, and profitability — while partly inflated by high nominal interest rates — has supported internal capital generation.

**Key themes for 2025/2026:**
- Monetary policy normalization: CBRT raised rates to 50%, compressing loan growth but supporting NIM
- Asset quality resilience: sector NPL ratio held below 3.5% despite macro headwinds
- FX exposure management: regulatory pressure has gradually reduced FX loan concentration
- Sovereign linkage: all FC ratings remain constrained by Turkey's B sovereign ceiling
- State bank divergence: private banks continue to outperform state-owned peers on capital and asset quality metrics

**Sector Outlook: STABLE**

### 1.2 Macro Credit Implications

**Inflation:** Elevated inflation has supported nominal loan growth and NIM expansion, but erodes real returns and increases operational cost pressures. CBRT's normalization path is constructive for medium-term stability.

**FX dynamics:** TRY depreciation continues to exert pressure on FX-denominated loan portfolios and wholesale funding costs. Banks with higher FX loan concentrations (state-owned peers) face disproportionate asset quality risk in adverse scenarios.

**GDP growth:** Resilient domestic demand supports loan origination and fee income. Construction and real estate remain key growth sectors, representing a systemic concentration risk.

## 3. SOVEREIGN LINKAGE & RATING CONSTRAINTS

All six banks in our coverage are rated at or below Turkey's sovereign rating of **B (Stable)**. This reflects Fitch's standard approach for operating environment constraints in emerging markets:

- **FC Long-Term IDR ceiling:** Turkey sovereign B
- **LC Long-Term IDR:** May be notched above sovereign for banks with strong LC franchise fundamentals
- **Viability Ratings:** Reflect standalone financial strength, independent of sovereign

**Key transmission channels:**
1. Asset quality: sovereign stress transmits to corporate and retail borrowers
2. Funding: sovereign rating affects wholesale funding costs and market access
3. FX: TRY volatility impacts FX loan portfolios and capital ratios
4. Regulatory: sovereign financial stress may prompt regulatory intervention

## 5. KEY RISKS & SENSITIVITIES

### Downside Risks
- **Renewed TRY depreciation** (>40% in 12 months): would pressure FX loan quality and capital ratios
- **Reversal of CBRT normalization:** re-acceleration of inflation would destabilize operating environment
- **Sovereign downgrade:** direct cap reduction on all bank FC ratings
- **Real estate market correction:** systemic exposure to construction sector

### Upside Sensitivities
- **Sovereign upgrade:** would unlock rating upside for all banks, with private banks benefiting first
- **Sustained disinflation:** improved macro predictability supports asset quality and profitability normalization
- **Current account improvement:** reduces external vulnerability and FX funding pressures

## 6. SECTOR OUTLOOK

**STABLE** — We expect Turkish banks to maintain broadly stable credit profiles over the next 12-18 months under our base case scenario. The monetary policy normalization path is constructive, capital buffers provide adequate shock absorption, and private bank asset quality remains resilient.

The primary constraint on positive rating momentum remains the sovereign ceiling. We will reassess sector ratings in conjunction with any sovereign rating action.

## APPENDIX: METHODOLOGY NOTE

This report applies a CAMELS-inspired analytical framework across five pillars:
- **Capital (25%):** CAR, CET1, leverage ratio
- **Asset Quality (25%):** NPL ratio, coverage ratio, cost of risk
- **Profitability (20%):** ROE, ROA, NIM, cost-to-income
- **Liquidity (15%):** LCR, loan-to-deposit ratio
- **FX Sensitivity (15%):** FX loan share, FX funding gap

Pillar scores (0-100) are aggregated into a composite score, which is mapped to an indicative rating category. Sovereign ceiling adjustments are applied separately.
