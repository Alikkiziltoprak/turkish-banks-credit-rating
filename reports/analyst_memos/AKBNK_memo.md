**3.2 Asset Quality — BEST IN CLASS**

**3.3 Profitability — STRONG**

## 4. WEAKNESSES & RISKS

**4.1 Sovereign Ceiling Constraint**
- Turkey sovereign rating (B, Stable) caps FC Long-Term IDR
- Standalone credit profile materially stronger than sovereign-constrained rating implies

**4.2 FX Loan Exposure**
- FX loans: 25.2% of total loan book
- Lower than sector average but still meaningful in TRY depreciation scenarios
- Interest rate risk from CBRT normalization cycle

**4.3 Concentration Risk**
- Single-market exposure: 100% Turkey
- Real estate and construction sector concentration — systemic risk in downturn
- Sabancı Holding concentration on shareholder side

**4.4 Macro Sensitivity**
- High inflation environment compresses real returns
- Geopolitical risk premium affects wholesale funding costs
- Current account deficit creates ongoing external vulnerability

## 7. OUTLOOK & RATING ACTION

### Base Case (65% probability)
- **Action:** Affirm at B / Stable Outlook

- **Trigger:** Continued macro stabilization, CBRT normalization on track

### Positive Case (25% probability)
- **Action:** Upgrade to B+ upon sovereign upgrade
- **Rationale:** Standalone profile is the strongest in coverage; any sovereign uplift flows directly to Akbank first

- **Rationale:** Akbank's strong buffers provide significant downside protection

## 8. ANALYST RECOMMENDATION

> **AFFIRM: B (Stable)**  
> Akbank presents the strongest quantitative credit profile in our Turkish banks coverage universe, with best-in-class capital adequacy, asset quality, and liquidity metrics. The sovereign ceiling is the sole binding constraint on the FC Long-Term IDR. Akbank would be the first beneficiary of any positive sovereign rating action. We recommend affirming at B/Stable with a positive sensitivity note tied explicitly to Turkey's sovereign trajectory.
