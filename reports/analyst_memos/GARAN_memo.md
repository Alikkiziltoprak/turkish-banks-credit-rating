## 3. STRENGTHS

**3.1 Capital Adequacy — STRONG**
- CAR: 17.2% (above sector average of ~15.5%)
- CET1: 14.1% — comfortable buffer above regulatory minimum (10.5%)
- BBVA parentage provides implicit capital support backstop

**3.2 Asset Quality — STRONG**
- NPL ratio: 2.1% — lowest among coverage universe
- Coverage ratio: 85% — robust provisioning relative to peers
- Conservative loan classification relative to state-owned banks

**3.3 Profitability — STRONG**
- ROE: 38.5% — sector-leading, driven by NIM expansion and fee income
- NIM: 5.2% — benefits from CBRT rate normalization cycle
- Cost-to-income: 28.5% — best-in-class operational efficiency

**3.4 Franchise & Strategic Support**
- BBVA ownership provides access to international funding markets
- Strong digital banking platform (Garanti BBVA Mobile: #1 in Turkey)
- Diversified revenue base: retail, SME, corporate, trade finance

---

## 4. WEAKNESSES & RISKS

**4.1 Sovereign Ceiling Constraint**
- Turkey sovereign rating (B, Stable) acts as ceiling on FC Long-Term IDR
- Material upside constrained regardless of standalone credit profile

**4.2 FX Loan Exposure**
- FX loans: 28.5% of total loan book
- USD appreciation scenarios create asset quality pressure in unhedged borrowers
- Partially mitigated by FX-indexed loan regulations (CBRT rules)

**4.3 Macro Environment**
- Inflation: ~65% (2024) — compresses real returns and operational predictability
- CBRT policy rate normalization ongoing — transition period creates NIM volatility
- Geopolitical risk premium embedded in Turkish assets broadly

**4.4 Concentration Risk**
- Single-market exposure: 100% Turkey — no geographic diversification
- Sector concentration in construction/real estate (historically elevated in Turkish banking)

## 7. OUTLOOK & RATING ACTION

### Base Case (60% probability)
- **Action:** Affirm at B / Stable Outlook
- **Rationale:** Solid fundamentals offset by sovereign ceiling constraint
- **Trigger:** Sovereign rating stability, continued NIM normalization

### Positive Case (25% probability)
- **Action:** Upgrade to B+ if sovereign upgraded
- **Rationale:** Standalone profile supports higher rating; sovereign ceiling is binding constraint
- **Trigger:** Turkey sovereign upgrade; sustained improvement in macro stability

### Negative Case (15% probability)
- **Action:** Negative Outlook / Downgrade Watch
- **Rationale:** Sharp TRY depreciation, NPL deterioration beyond 5%, capital erosion
- **Trigger:** Renewed currency crisis, reversal of CBRT normalization

## 8. ANALYST RECOMMENDATION

> **AFFIRM: B (Stable)**  
> Garanti BBVA demonstrates the strongest standalone credit profile in our Turkish banks coverage universe. Capital buffers are robust, asset quality metrics are sector-leading, and BBVA parentage provides a meaningful strategic anchor. However, the sovereign ceiling remains the binding constraint on the FC Long-Term IDR. We recommend affirming at B/Stable, with a positive sensitivity to any sovereign rating action.
